Eiffel Tower — Key Facts
The Eiffel Tower is an iron lattice tower located on the Champ de Mars in Paris, France.
Completed in 1889 for the Exposition Universelle (1889 World's Fair).
Designed by engineer Gustave Eiffel and his company.
Height: originally 300 m (984 ft); with antennas its height is about 324 m (1,063 ft).
Constructed from wrought iron and consists of 18,038 pieces joined by 2.5 million rivets.
It was the tallest man-made structure in the world until 1930.
Sources: historical records and engineering documentation.
